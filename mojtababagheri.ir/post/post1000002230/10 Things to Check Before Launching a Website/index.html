Blog/ 10 Things to Check Before Launching a Website
12 November 1400
Mojtaba Bagheri
Web Designer
62

12 November 1400

Successfully launching a website is not an easy task. Almost inevitably, just when it looks like you're ready to go live, it turns out that your enthusiasm got the better of you and you almost released something incomplete. Even a small mistake can change the way people view a website and related products. And sure, if the site is for yourself, you can tweak it later, but if you're going to produce one for a client, you need to make sure everything runs smoothly from day one. that even the smallest mistakes are noticed before they become big problems.

Here I found some useful ways to make sure.

1. Check out the different

browsers

This one cannot be stressed enough. Always check that everything looks and works in multiple browsers. Because, even if a site looks perfect when using Chrome, it might look like complete crap elsewhere, so never assume anything on that front.

2. Don't forget titles and metadata

Your page title is important not only for SEO reasons, but also because it gives future visitors a first look at what they can actually find, so set a meta title for each page before launching and Make sure it's relevant. Page Content Try adding metadata such as descriptions or tags. While they aren't as important to SEO as meta titles, a good meta description invites more users to check out a specific page, and by tagging it correctly, you make it easier to find.

data collection

3. Check out the links

Never assume that links work. Maybe you made a spelling mistake, or when you inserted the link in the text, you accidentally deleted it instead of pasting the URL, and as a result your link is nothing more than randomly underlined text? Believe it or not, even advanced webmasters make these seemingly beginner mistakes, so double check. And internal and external links are vital, because successfully linking your page to a trusted partner domain may help you attract thousands of customers.

4. Test performance

The ease with which a user can navigate a site is very important. If you plan to offer customers the ability to submit complaints via a contact form, for example, test it yourself, see if it works, and then test again. If you want to invite them to buy, check if the sales system is properly integrated with the rest of the site. We suggest that you do all these things yourself. After all, probably no one knows more about your target customer's goals and experiences than you do. Also consider supporting these findings with user testing: you will find many different platforms offering testing services on the Internet, and getting positive or negative feedback from an independent user(s) can be very important.

5. Validation

Get the site rsquo;s code before launching Valid HTML, according to standards set by the Wide Web Consortium. If your problem doesn't work, it could mean that there are some serious problems, and therefore, it is up to you to find and fix them. Keep in mind that not being verified doesn't mean you didn't win, but it could indicate a variety of issues that could potentially degrade the user experience.

6. Analysis

Installing a proper analytics tool is a great idea. These help measure important statistics such as unique visitors, conversions and page views, and notify you when the numbers start to drop. Google Analytics is the most popular tool and is also available for free, but paid programs such as Clicky or Statcounter can provide more in-depth data. Regardless of how they are collected, the resulting statistics can be the key to launching a successful marketing campaign.

7. Includes site map

Always remember to add a sitemap.xml file to your main directory, which will help search engines index your site in a faster and more efficient way. You don't even have to do it yourself, because tools like XML-Sitemaps can do it without your intervention. A site mapto create Alternatively, in WordPress, you can install Google XML Sitemap Plugin. Creating one is quick and simple enough, and in today's world, it's almost a must if you want a successful outcome.

8. Has a creative "404 error" screen

Error 404 or ldquo;page rsquo;t exists rdquo; The answer is one of the most common errors found during browsing. Whenever a user tries to access a non-existent page, that page is displayed. However, don't just settle for a simple and basic message, because you can learn how to customize an ldquo;error 404 rdquo; It will be seen on your website. By offering them suggestions they might be looking for, or simply letting them return to the home page.

9. Configure for optimal performance

Optimization is very important and allows the site to rank higher in search engine results, attract new audiences and simply work properly. A good way to do this is to minimize HTML requests or use CSS sprites as much as possible. Also, make sure each page loads smoothly and completely, with no missing elements or annoying (and unskippable) banner ads covering important content. And remember that optimizing a site doesn't just happen once: you have to repeat the process several times after launch.

10. Correction

Ensuring high-quality textual content is just as important to user satisfaction as ensuring that all technical aspects work properly. That's why whenever you're writing an article, blog post, or anything else, take the time to review it, including whether it's easy to read. Ask a friend or family member to do the same, then ask them to share their thoughts. You can make the process easier by avoiding long sentences and paragraphs, adding headings, and emphasizing easy-to-understand language.
