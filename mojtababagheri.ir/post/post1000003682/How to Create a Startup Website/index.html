Having a website is as essential to a successful startup as a business card was a few decades ago. But creating a website for your organization is only half the battle. Beyond what a business card used to do, your website is not only a place to share contact information and get prospects to call, it's also where you inspire users, engage them, and start converting. They give to the customer.

Do it yourself or hire a professional?

One of the main decisions small business owners have to make is whether to hire professional help or build their own website. DIY website builders and templates are great for personal blogs and their functionality is constantly improving. First of all, they are also a cheaper option than working with a professional web designer. However, they have a few drawbacks.

Limited creativity

Your creativity is limited by the number of designs in your package. While not impossible, you may struggle to achieve a close fit with your brand. This can lead to problems in brand stability.

Search Engine Optimization (SEO)

SEO is vital to getting your business found by search engines, but it can be done without expert support at first. Achieving great rankings also means updating regularly.

Time commitment


As a founder, you are already spread thin. If you are not an expert in website design, can you afford to spend a lot of your time creating a website? It may save you money today, but it will probably cost you in the long run.

Working with a professional or marketing agency allows you to focus on your existing priorities. Your web design and UI/UX will be customized and on-brand, increasing your site's chances of standing out from the crowd.

How professionals approach the website development process

Marketing agencies or professional web designers have a strategic process. The workflow varies from agency to agency, but often includes project scoping, strategy creation, and development of site structure and framework.

  • Project Scope: Project scoping means gathering information about your business and its competitors. One of the most important things you need to determine is what your website should do for your business.
  • Strategy: Considering the target audience and keywords can be part of this step, as well as thinking about how users move around the site.
  • Site Structure: Time to think about content. While Google will rank websites that provide their users with quality and problem-solving content, more is not always better. Consider what is important to your brand and stick to it.
  • Wireframe: Also known as mockup design, this is one of the first creative steps to help you get a feel for your site layout. If you're taking the DIY approach, this is often the first thing you'll notice about your pattern. Once you start moving the drag and drop elements around, you'll start to adjust the look, but there will always be limits to your creativity.

closer to the website design process

With website development well underway, it's time to consider key areas of website design, including:

  • Brand guidelines.
  • Goals and strategy.
  • User experiences (UI/UX).
  • Visual appearance and content.

A successful startup needs a website that is visually more beautiful. Your organization's website should reflect your brand. This means that anyone who has visited your social media accounts or met with your team in person should instantly recognize it as yours.

User experience may have become a bit of a buzzword, but it couldn't be more important. Creating a positive user experience starts with building a website that loads quickly. Statistics show that pages that load within five seconds have the highest conversion rates. p gt;

In addition to being fast, your site needs to be optimized for mobile devices. Today, the majority of website traffic comes from mobile phones, and according to Google, 60% of all mobile users Contacted a business directly from mobile search results. As a new business, you cannot miss these opportunities.

Your website rsquo;s structure and content are very important to its performance in search engines. Text remains the main basis of searchand makes it the focus of SEO work. However, remember the value of other assets on your website: descriptive text around images and graphics helps them rank. Clear calls to action (CTAs) make it easy for your customers to understand what you want them to do.

The visual appearance of your site should match your brand image. It's not worth compromising your brand to fit a DIY template. On the other hand, you may be able to create a template that allows you to follow brand guidelines while still offering creative options.

Choosing between the professional route or the DIY route when creating your business website involves several considerations. Time, cost, technical know-how and more all play a role in the decision.

What should not be debated is whether or not your business needs an outstanding website. A well-designed and well-functioning website is critical to the success of a small business or startup. It may seem like an afterthought when you're busy building all the other parts of the organization, but it can make the difference between securing or not securing critical early customers and convincing investors for your next round of funding.
